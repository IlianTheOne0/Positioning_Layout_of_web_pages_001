What is the History of Halloween?

Halloween’s origins date back to the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain (pronounced sow-in). The Celts, who lived 2,000 years ago, mostly in the area that is now Ireland, the United Kingdom and northern France, celebrated their new year on November 1.

This day marked the end of summer and the harvest and the beginning of the dark, cold winter, a time of year that was often associated with human death. Celts believed that on the night before the new year, the boundary between the worlds of the living and the dead became blurred. On the night of October 31 they celebrated Samhain, when it was believed that the ghosts of the dead returned to earth.

When it comes to spooky celebrations, two names often come to mind: Halloween and Helloween. While they might sound similar, these two occasions have distinct characteristics and histories. Halloween is a widely recognized holiday with a rich history, while Helloween is a more recent concept that has gained popularity in certain circles.

Traditions

  • Jack-o’-Lanterns

    The tradition of carving Jack-o’-Lanterns originated in Ireland using turnips instead of pumpkins. It is allegedly based on a legend about a man named Stingy Jack who repeatedly trapped the Devil and only let him go on the condition that Jack would never go to Hell. But when Jack died, he learned that Heaven did not want his soul either, so he was forced to wander the Earth as a ghost for eternity. The Devil gave Jack a burning lump of coal in a carved-out turnip to light his way. Locals eventually began carving scary faces into their own turnips to frighten away evil spirits.

  • Wearing Scary Costumes

    In order to avoid being terrorized by all the evil spirits walking the Earth during Samhain, the Celts donned disguises in order to confuse the spirits and be left alone.

  • Trick-or-Treating

    Trick-or-treating may have origins in three traditions: Celtic Samhain, where people dressed as spirits for food; Scottish "guising," where children collected treats in exchange for songs or tricks; and German-American "belsnickeling," where disguised children received treats if neighbors couldn’t guess their identities.

  • Easy Halloween cookies

    A bright, bubbling, green potion that's sure to catch everyone's eye.

    • 1 liter lime soda
    • 1 liter ginger ale
    • 2 cups pineapple juice
    • 3-4 scoops lime sherbet
    • gummy worms for garnish (optional)
    1. In a large punch bowl, combine lime soda, ginger ale, and pineapple juice.
    2. Add scoops of lime sherbet to create a frothy, bubbling effect.
    3. Toss in some gummy worms for extra creepiness.

  • Mummy Hot Dogs

    Fun, easy, and perfect for a spooky snack!

    • 1 can crescent roll dough
    • 8 hot dogs
    • Mustard or ketchup for eyes
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. Cut dough into thin strips and wrap around each hot dog, leaving a space for the "face."
    3. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until golden brown.
    4. Dot mustard or ketchup for eyes on each "mummy."

  • Poison Apple Cocktails

    A dark, mysterious drink with an apple twist.

    • 1 cup apple cider
    • 1 oz black vodka (or add black food coloring to regular vodka)
    • 1/2 oz grenadine
    • Ice
    • Black sugar for rimming (optional)
    1. Rim the glass with black sugar if desired.
    2. Pour apple cider into a glass filled with ice.
    3. Slowly add grenadine, followed by vodka, to layer the colors.
    4. Stir slightly to create a dark, swirling look.

  • Spider Web Dip

    A layered dip with a "web" of sour cream, perfect for chips and veggies.

    • 1 can refried beans
    • 1 cup guacamole
    • 1 cup salsa
    • 1/2 cup sour cream
    • 1/4 cup shredded cheese
    • 1/4 cup chopped tomatoes
    • 1/4 cup sliced olives
    • 1 small plastic spider for decoration (optional)
    1. Spread refried beans in the base of a dish.
    2. Layer with guacamole, then salsa.
    3. Add a cheese layer, tomatoes, and olives.
    4. Put sour cream in a piping bag and create a spider web pattern on top.
    5. Add a plastic spider for a fun garnish.
